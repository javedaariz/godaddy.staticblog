Cheapest Coastal Towns for a Family Weekend in the UK

Explore stunning seaside escapes without stretching your budget.

Dreaming of sea air and family fun without draining your wallet? The UK coastline is full of affordable gems where your pounds stretch further than you think. Here’s a guide to the best cheap seaside towns where families can enjoy a full weekend on a modest budget.

1. Oban, Scotland – Gateway to the Isles

Topping the affordability chart, Oban on Scotland’s west coast combines dramatic scenery with unbeatable value. Known as the “Gateway to the Isles,” this harbour town offers fresh seafood, cliffside walks, and ferry trips to Mull or Kerrera for under £10 per person. Family B&Bs start at £45 per night, with most attractions free to enjoy.

2. Morecambe, Lancashire – Vintage Seaside Charm

Morecambe captures classic British seaside joy at a fraction of the cost. Its long promenade, Art Deco buildings, and family parks make it ideal for all ages. A beachfront stay often costs under £70, and Happy Mount Park’s mini golf and splash zones keep kids entertained without overspending.

3. Blackpool – Budget Fun and Bright Lights

Blackpool remains one of the UK’s most affordable family-friendly beaches. Enjoy free walks along the promenade, the Illuminations, and endless penny arcades. Typical stays start from £50 per night, with discounted entry to key attractions like the Tower Eye widely available online.

4. Troon, Ayrshire – Scotland’s Relaxed Seaside Haven

Troon is a tranquil escape featuring clean sands and scenic sunset walks. Just 40 minutes from Glasgow, its relaxed atmosphere and £60-per-night guesthouses make it a true family favourite. Local fish suppers and ice cream stalls invite you to linger by the sea.

5. Filey, North Yorkshire – Classic Seaside Quietude

Peaceful and underrated, Filey brings golden sands and cliff walks without the high price tags. Beach chalets rent for under £20 a day, with family accommodation averaging £70 a night. Simple seaside pleasures thrive here — from sandcastles to generous portions at cosy cafés.

6. Withernsea, East Yorkshire – Hidden Budget Gem

Withernsea’s low-cost stays and relaxed pace make it one of Yorkshire’s hidden gems. Explore its long beach, picnic by the sea, or visit the Withernsea Lighthouse Museum for just a few pounds. Ideal for a quiet family weekend away.

7. Hartlepool, County Durham – Affordable Coastal Break with Character

Hartlepool blends history, coastline, and affordability. The marina and National Royal Navy Museum are highlights — the latter offering free admission. Add local fish and chips, and it’s a family escape worth revisiting.

8. Clacton-on-Sea, Essex – Fun and Frugal

Just two hours from London, Clacton offers old-school seaside excitement. Rides from £1, affordable guesthouses under £60, and free sandy beaches make it an ideal budget getaway for families looking for sun and smiles.

9. Fleetwood, Lancashire – Peaceful and Pocket-Friendly

Fleetwood’s tranquil harbour and vintage landmarks offer a budget-friendly haven near Blackpool. B&B stays can cost as little as £55 per night. Families can explore Marine Hall Gardens or the local market for relaxed, affordable fun.

10. Why Affordability Matters

Choosing affordable coastal towns doesn’t just save money — it helps support local communities, keeps travel sustainable, and opens the door to authentic British seaside experiences. From Withernsea’s quiet charms to Blackpool’s buzzing lights, family seaside breaks can be priceless without being pricey.
